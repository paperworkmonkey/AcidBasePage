Stewart variables
Independent variables PCO2 Lungs
ATOT Liver Total weak non-volatile acids
SID Kidneys Strong ion difference
Dependent variables H+
OH-
HCO3-
CO32-
HA Weak acid
A- Weak anions
  • SIDa = apparent SID = charge difference between measures strong ions and strong anions. Normal values 40-46. SIDa = (Na+ + K+ + Ca2+ + Mg2+) - (Cl− + Lactate−)
  • SIDe = Effective SID = calculated sum of charges measured weak acids in plasma (bic, albumin and phosphate).
    SIDe = Bic- + Albumin- + Phosphate-
    Normal value is 36-40.
  • SIG = SIDa – SIDe. i.e. take the SIDa (calculated from difference in charges on strong cations and anions) and subtract the charges from weak acids (SIDe) - what is left is a caused by known anions (e.g. sulphate, acetate, citrate, D-lactate etc.).
    • Increased SIG acidosis:
      1. ketoacidosis
      2. D-lactate
      3. uraemia
      4. hepatic failure
      5. toxins ( (organic alcohols, iron, isoniazid, cyanide paraldehyde, salicylates))
    • Decreased SIG acidosis:
      1. lithium toxicity
      2. bromine toxicity
      3. THAM
      4. paraproteins

Stewart equations

  1. [H+] × [OH−] = K′w (water dissociation equilibrium)
  2. [H+] × [A−] = KA × [HA] (weak acid equilibrium)
  3. [HA] + [A−] = [ATOT] (conservation of mass for “A”)
  4. [H+] × [HCO3−] = KC × pCO2 (bicarbonate ion formation equilibrium)
  5. [H+] × [CO32−] = K3 × [HCO3−] (carbonate ion formation equilibrium)
  6. [SID] + [H+] − [HCO3−] − [A−] − [CO32−] − [OH−] = 0 (maintenance of electrical neutrality)
